Pan-Seared Filet Mignon

Ingredients

  • 1 filet mignon steak (about 6 oz)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for searing)
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2-3 sprigs fresh thyme (or rosemary)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black peper, to taste

Optional (for a richer flavor):

  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon freshly squeezed lemon juice

Description

This rich and flavorful filet mignon is perfectly seared to create a golden crust, locking in its tender, juicy center. Infused with fragrant garlic and fresh herbs, itâ€™s finished with a touch of butter for a velvety, luxurious taste. A hint of white wine vinegar and lemon adds brightness, enhancing the savory flavors. This elegant steak is perfect for a special dinner or a simple yet indulgent meal at home.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Steak
    • Season the filet generously with salt, freshly ground black pepper, smoked paprika, and a pinch of garlic powder.
    • Let it sit for 15 minutes to absorb the seasoning.

  2. Heat the Skillet
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a cast-iron skillet over high heat until shimmering.

  3. Sear the Filet
    • Place the steak in the hot skillet, pressing it down lightly.
    • Sear for 3 minutes on each side until a deep brown crust forms.

  4. Add Aromatics
    • Lower the heat to medium, add 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, crushed garlic cloves, and fresh thyme or rosemary.
    • Spoon the sizzling butter and herbs over the steak, basting it for 1-2 minutes.

  5. Enhance Flavor (Optional)
    • Add a splash of white wine vinegar and a squeeze of lemon juice to the pan for a tangy finish.
    • Let the liquids bubble and reduce for 30 seconds, then swirl them around.

  6. Rest and Serve
    • Remove the steak from the skillet and let it rest for 5 minutes.
    • Slice and savor the herb-infused, perfectly cooked filet.
