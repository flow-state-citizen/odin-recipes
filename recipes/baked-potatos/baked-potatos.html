Baked Potatos

Ingredients

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2-3 sprigs fresh thyme (or rosemary)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Sour cream or Greek yogurt (for topping)
  • Crispy bacon bits (for topping)
  • Shredded cheese (for topping)
  • Chives (optional)
  • Hot sauce (optional)

Description

This classic baked potato is tender on the inside with a perfectly crispy, seasoned skin. It’s baked until fluffy, then finished with a drizzle of butter, garlic, and fresh thyme for added richness. The simplicity of salt and pepper allows the natural flavors to shine, while the oven creates the ideal texture. Whether served alongside grilled meats, roasted vegetables, or enjoyed on its own, this baked potato is a comforting and versatile dish perfect for any meal.

For extra indulgence, top it off with a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt for creaminess and sprinkle crispy bacon bits for a salty crunch. Other great toppings include shredded cheese, chives, or even a touch of hot sauce for a kick. These additions turn the humble baked potato into a satisfying, crowd-pleasing side or hearty main course.

Steps

  1. Prepare the Potatos
    • Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with foil.
    • Scrub the potatos, pat them dry, and poke a few holes in each with a fork
    • Rub each potato with olive oil, sprinkle generously with salt and pepper, and place them directly on the oven rack.

  2. Bake the Potatos
    • Bake for 45-60 minutes, or until the skins are crispy and the inside is tender when pierced with a fork.

  3. Make the Garlic Butter
    • While the potatoes are baking, melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat.
    • Add the minced garlic and thyme sprigs, and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Remove from heat.

  4. Finish the Potatos
    • Once baked, remove the potatoes from the oven and carefully slice them open lengthwise.
    • Drizzle the garlic thyme butter over the inside of each potato.

  5. Top and Serve
    • Add a dollop of sour cream or Greek yogurt, then sprinkle bacon bits, shredded cheese, chives, or hot sauce as desired.
    • Serve hot and enjoy!
